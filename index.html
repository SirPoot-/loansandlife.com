Federal Housing Administration (FHA)

A mortgage that’s insured by the Federal Housing Administration (FHA). They are popular especially among first time home buyers because they allow down payments of 3.5% for credit scores of 580+. However, borrowers must pay mortgage insurance premiums, which protects the lender if a borrower defaults.

Conventional

Originated and serviced by private mortgage lenders like banks, credit unions and other financial institutions, many of which also offer government-insured mortgage loans. In general, conventional loans don't have some of the same perks as government-insured loans, such as low credit score requirements and no down payment or mortgage insurance.

Veterans Affairs (VA) Loan

$0 down mortgage option available to Veterans, Service Members and select military spouses. VA loans are issued by private lenders, such as a mortgage company or bank, and guaranteed by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA).

Refinance

Process of getting a new loan for your home. When you refinance, the new mortgage loan pays off the old one, so you’re left with just one loan and one monthly payment.

Cash Out

Option gives homeowners the opportunity to turn some of the equity they have built up in their home into cash.

Rate and Term

Homeowners who are simply interested in changing the interest rate and term of their mortgage, without adjusting their loan amount.

Reverse Mortgage

Homeowners 62 and older may be eligible for the many benefits of a reverse mortgage.

Home Renovation

Using equity in your hole to finance a new pool, kitchen, or bathroom while rates are low.

Final Expense and Burial Costs (FEX)

Simplified Whole Life Insurance suited to cover the cost of burial, cremation, and funeral costs. Final Expense Life Insurance is a family planning tool designed to protect and benefit your loved ones when you pass away. When an individual passes, a family is faced with a great deal of work and numerous bills and expenses. These sudden expenses can be very difficult and stressful for a family to coordinate and take care of, especially when the family is at the early stage of emotionally dealing with the loss. Final Expense Life Insurance enables your loved ones to have a clear plan to handle these costs, allowing an appropriate and affective time and environment to come to peace and celebrate the life of their loved one. Final Expense is always a type of whole life insurance which is a form of permanent life insurance. Unlike term insurance, which only pays off if you die during the “term” of the policy, a whole life policy remains in effect for your entire life, guaranteeing that the benefits will be there no matter how long you live (as long as you continue to pay your premiums).

Mortgage Protection (MTG)

The number one investment for families is the purchase of a home. The equity you build by owning your home could be tens of thousands of dollars. Equity is the amount you would be able to keep and put in your pocket (bank account) after selling your home and paying off the balance of your mortgage. Mortgage Protection is essentially Term Life Insurance with the goal of protecting the ownership in your home upon your passing or if you health deteriorates to a point of disability. You may be the breadwinner in the family or even the loss of income of a couple could put you in an irrecoverable position. Protecting your home and equity is possible in two ways.
  1. Having enough coverage in place for your loved ones to pay off the home to keep it and lower the monthly costs associated with mortgage payments.
  2. Common misconception is that you need to have coverage amounting to more than you owe on your mortgage to pay it off when you die. In reality, you could also take a whole life (permanent life) Insurance policy out in the amount of even $10,000. By using that $10,000 to cover your mortgage payments for an extended portion of time (6 months – 3 years), it will give your loved the ones the opportunity to grieve and decide on what do with the house. They may decide they can afford to keep and continue to make the payments on it. Decide to sell it, which that coverage amount will provide the time arrange to move into a new place or if it takes several months for the property to sell, then it that time frame is covered as well. The ultimate goal here is to protect the equity in the home. If the home sells, then the equity will go back to your loved one. The worst case scenario is that no coverage is in place, the house can’t be maintained or sold in time and the bank forecloses on the home. They take your equity and the years and years of mortgage payments paid into the home are gone.
